Tiger Facts

Tigers have so many wonderful aspects about their lives and features that everyone should know more about them. This page is where you get to learn many wonderful, fascinating information and facts about tigers. If you wanna learn more, there is a handy Nat Geo Kids Video on some tiger facts!

-Patterned skin-

Image from Twitter from the Dallas Zoo.

It is quite obvious that tigers have vibrant orange fur with black, slender stripes. However, did you know that their pattern is imprinted onto their skin as well? It's almost like a human's fingerprint which marks who they are. (Imagine being born with a "tattoo")

-Sanitizing Saliva-

Something I found very fascinating about tigers is the fact they have antiseptic saliva. You read that right. Their saliva is almost like a disinfectant preventing the spread of bacteria. They usually lick themselves to prevent infection which is almost like healing powers in my opinion.

-Variety Diet-

Tigers are known to eat just about ANY animal. It does not matter if it's a bird, rhino, buffalo, or even another large wildcat. If they get in the tigers way, they will become deadmeat. (Literally)

-Vulnerable Little Cubs-

Unfortunately, all tiger cubs are born blind. Because of this, there are many cubs that do not survive. They are mainly relying on the scent of their mother to guide them but might end up dying of starvation or eaten by a male tiger for mating with the female. (Poor things!)

-Scented Urine-

I had to save the most fascinating yet hilarious fact for the end of all this. Tiger urine smells like buttered popcorn. That is a very interesting smell to resemble. The urine smell also poses as a warning to intruders in their surroundings. (such an interesting comparison.)
